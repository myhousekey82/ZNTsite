Pricing

The association pays nothing net. Owners fund the process.

Collection costs are assessed directly to the delinquent owner's account. As balances are recovered, the association is reimbursed. No retainers, no upfront fees, no permanent cost to the HOA.

How the money moves.

The model is transparent by design. Every dollar that flows out comes back.

Step 1: Zero Net Thirty completes collection work and invoices the association monthly for steps taken. Step 2: The association pays Zero Net Thirty. Those charges are simultaneously assessed to the delinquent owner's account. Step 3: As the owner pays down their balance, the association is reimbursed. Net cost to the association: zero.
  • Monthly invoicing for collection steps completed, no retainer or upfront engagement fee
  • Every charge invoiced to the association is assessed against the owner's account
  • The association is reimbursed as the owner pays down their balance
  • Net cost to the HOA is zero, owners fund the process
  • Legal escalation only when warranted, protecting the association's recoverable balance
  • All collection charges logged in your management software so boards see exactly what has been assessed and why
Common questions

Questions we hear most.

If you don't see your question here, ask us directly. We respond fast.

How do you handle court representation without being a law firm?
Zero Net Thirty is authorized to represent associations directly in Texas Justice of the Peace Court proceedings, without the cost or timeline delays of a traditional attorney engagement. Legal counsel is only engaged when an account requires district court action, where attorney representation is required.
What happens if an owner disputes the debt?
Disputed accounts are reviewed against the association's governing documents and account ledger. We work with the management team to ensure the balance is accurately documented before any escalation. Owners have a clear channel to raise questions and our process is designed to handle disputes professionally without stalling the overall timeline.
What if you can't collect on an account?
Every account is pursued through our full escalation process, including JP Court when necessary. When a judgment is obtained and the owner does not pay, we file an abstract of judgment, preserving the association's claim and attaching it to the owner's property. Hardship situations may extend the timeline, but they don't extinguish the association's right to collect. The association's interest is protected at every stage.
Do you work with self-managed HOAs?
Zero Net Thirty is built primarily for professional HOA management companies. Self-managed communities are welcome to reach out, but our integration-based workflow works best alongside a management software platform. For communities operating outside of that environment, we'd discuss what's possible on a case-by-case basis.
How long does onboarding take?
Onboarding is straightforward. We work within your existing management software environment and there's no new system to implement on your end. We'll meet with your accounting team to map GL accounts and conduct a portfolio health check to establish a baseline. After that initial groundwork, we can typically be operational within a few weeks.
What states do you currently serve?
We are currently operating in Austin, Texas. Our agency model and small claims process is built around Texas HOA statutes and court procedures. We plan to expand to additional cities and states. If you're outside of Austin, Texas, reach out and we'll let you know our timeline.
How are boards kept informed?
Account status and collection charges are logged directly in your management software, giving boards visibility into every account without requesting a separate report. Management companies can also grant board members view access to collections activity through their existing management software permissions.
Is there a minimum portfolio size to work with you?
No minimum. We work with HOA management companies managing a single community and companies managing hundreds. The process scales to any portfolio size, and smaller portfolios often see the most dramatic percentage improvement in delinquency rates.
